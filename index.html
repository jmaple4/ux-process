AWS APO UX design process

0. Customer feedback

We seek ongoing customers feedback on jobs to be done through direct data and generative research.

Time frame

Year-round

Data sources
  • Voice of customer (VOC) tickets
  • Post-launch product feedback
  • Generative research studies
  • CX Marketing research studies
  • OP1/2 process

1. Organization

PM, engineering, & UX start the design process with data, business requirements and scope evaluation.

Time frame

2 weeks - 1 month

Key tasks & artifacts

  • 5Qs written
  • PM requirements drafted
  • PR/FAQ started
  • Intake forms completed

Completion milestone

  • Design & research assigned

2. Discovery

We gather insights into problems and define how we solve them based on data.

Time frame

1 - 3 months

Key tasks & artifacts

  • UX kick-off with stakeholders
  • Analyze existing research and tickets
  • Stakeholder workshops
  • Full scope and requirements defined
  • PR/FAQ completed
  • UX generative research
  • Heuristic analysis of current UX
  • Flow diagrams of proposed UX
  • API & engineering feasibility review

Completion milestone

  • Milestone review: Deep dive

3. Design

Solutions are designed, tested, and approved.
We find what works and agree to build it.

Time frame

2 - 3 months

Key tasks & artifacts

  • Flow diagrams, journey maps, & lo-fi designs
  • Lo-fi prototype testing
  • Engineering feasibility review
  • Hi-fi designs
  • Hi-fi prototype testing

Completion milestone

  • Milestone review: UX Sign-off

4. Build

Engineering builds designed solutions. UX supports implementation
edge cases and finds workarounds.

Time frame

2 - 3 months

Key tasks & artifacts

  • Engineering builds UX designs
  • Edge cases solved with design
  • Go-to-market plan crafted

Completion milestone

  • Milestone review: UX Fit & finish

5. Launch

We ship the new customer experience,
spin the flywheel, and evaluate the impact.

Time frame

2 - 3 months

Completion milestone

  • Products & features launch

Post launch key tasks

  • KPIs analyzed
  • Deprioritized features tracked
  • Go-to-market plan crafted
